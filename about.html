The Journey of Diyaa (dhad)

From a simple donut tutorial to exploring the deep complexity of AI and OS architecture.

Driven by Complexity and Service.

I am a highly motivated Developer and Content Creator specializing in active learning. My passion lies in diving deep into challenging systems—whether building a complex game mechanic in Godot, structuring a full-stack application, or optimizing an Arch Linux build. My ultimate goal is to apply this drive to Artificial Intelligence and Computer Science to create tools that serve and empower millions.

Core Technologies

  • Content: YouTube (Instructional Design), DaVinci Resolve, Premiere Pro
  • Programming: Python, C# (Basic), Dart (Flutter), GDScript (Godot)
  • Game/3D: Godot, Unity, UPBGE, Blender, Inkscape
  • OS & Systems: Arch Linux, Fedora (Gnome), Windows 11, Shell Scripting

Modern Development Philosophy: Focus on the Idea

The rise of AI has redefined the role of the modern developer. My experience with various technologies—from game engines to operating systems—is now augmented by advanced tools, which I embrace as a significant asset.

My central principle is that the value lies in the concept, not the keystroke. AI acts as a force multiplier, offloading technical scaffolding and boilerplate code. This frees my creative bandwidth to focus intensely on:

  • **Conceptual Design:** Solving complex user problems with novel, efficient ideas.
  • **User Experience (UX):** Ensuring the final product is intuitive, useful, and delightful.
  • **Architectural Integrity:** Structuring systems to be robust, scalable, and maintainable.

This approach allows me to leverage the full spectrum of modern capabilities, raising the bar for production quality and accelerating the journey from a complex idea to a functional reality.

Learning Philosophy: Intentional Risk-Taking

My growth is fueled by deliberately taking on problems that are beyond my current comfort zone. I enjoy pushing ideas until I reach the **limits of my understanding**, even if that means hitting technical walls or leaving a project unfinished. Those moments of resistance are where I gain the most valuable knowledge.

AI has made this approach far more practical. By accelerating prototyping, reducing boilerplate, and helping me explore unfamiliar tools quickly, AI allows me to attempt ambitious ideas that would normally be out of reach at my current stage. When I do encounter real constraints—such as system boundaries or environment limitations—I treat them as **valuable learning points** rather than failures.

This combination of rapid experimentation and intentional risk-taking keeps building both challenging and enjoyable, and it helps me grow faster than staying within safe, predictable solutions.

Timeline: A Path Forged by Curiosity

2014-2016

The Donut and the Dive

My journey began at age 10-11 in Jordan with Blender 3D. Following the "Donut Tutorial" taught me not just 3D modeling, but the Windows environment and the smooth use of a keyboard. This simple start led to my first creative outlet: video creation using Movavi Video Editor, primarily focusing on comedy and teaching simple Blender techniques in Arabic. This phase established my earliest motivation: active learning and production.

2017-2019

From UPBGE to Linux Systems

I discovered the UPBGE game engine, finding a new passion: creating games. This led me to quickly teach myself Inkscape (vector graphics) for better video thumbnails. The search for capability eventually pulled me toward Godot (requiring Python) and Unity (requiring C#), recognizing the limitations of basic tools. Concurrently, technical frustration with Windows led me down the rabbit hole of Linux distribution, culminating in a successful, yet challenging, build of Arch Linux—a testament to my love for overcoming complexity.

2020-2023

High School, Complexity, and Purpose

High school introduced me to challenges I craved. A physics teacher presented me with the complexity of university-level material, making simple exams feel boring. I realized I thrive when challenged to the point of failure. This period solidified my internal drive: I exist in peace for those who need me. My high school experience became less about grades (though I achieved 96.88%) and more about mentoring, supporting, and sharing the excitement of knowledge with my peers.

2024-Present

The Focus: AI and Helping at Scale

After a mental low caused by losing purpose, I rebuilt my faith and rediscovered my drive. Now, I focus on the smallest, most perfectible step. My recent work with Gemini CLI and building custom HTML/Python apps (like the scholarship tracker) demonstrates my commitment to using programming for practical, organizational solutions. My ultimate goal is to pursue AI/CS because I see it as the ultimate tool to help people, enabling assistance and guidance on a massive scale.

The Core Drive

My Inner Reason

My passion for AI/CS goes beyond complexity. I see it as the ultimate way to help millions—creating an intelligent tool that can protect, teach, and assist others globally. Making that AI module basically equals helping millions.

Overcoming Setbacks

I experienced my hardest mental moment when I lost my YouTube purpose. I overcame this by developing a specialized faith: focusing only on the next small, perfectible move and believing that this small, high-quality effort will eventually lead to major achievements.

Lesson Learned

I don't really need to worry about the big, final outcome. I only have to worry about the quality and intention of my immediate, small steps. Perfection in the present moment is the only real goal.
